OVERVIEW

Since the outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic, forecasting future case trends has become crucial for governments, healthcare providers, and policy planners. This project focuses on predicting the trajectory of COVID-19 cases using SARIMAX (Seasonal AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average with eXogenous variables), a time series modeling technique that incorporates both seasonal trends and external influencing factors. By analyzing historical COVID-19 data along with relevant external variables such as mobility patterns, vaccination rates, and government interventions, our model provides accurate and insightful predictions. These forecasts can help authorities make timely decisions related to healthcare resource allocation, lockdown policies, and public safety measures.

Our interactive web platform visualizes the predicted trends and allows users to explore future scenarios based on current data. This project demonstrates the power of data-driven forecasting in managing public health crises and preparing for potential future outbreaks.
